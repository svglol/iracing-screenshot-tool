## Summary

v1.4 Tooling Modernization delivered the full modernization of the ESLint + TypeScript + Babel toolchain without touching runtime Electron/Vue code. The milestone:

- Retired two deprecated Babel packages (`babel-runtime` + `babel-eslint`) per Phase 5
- Migrated ESLint 7 → 9 with `eslint.config.js` flat config, `eslint-config-prettier` 9 → 10, and full `plugin:prettier/recommended` wiring (superseding v1.3 Phase 4 Pitfall 4 derogation) per Phase 6
- Upgraded TypeScript 3.8 → 5.7 + `@typescript-eslint/*` 2.25 → 8.x and closed with `--legacy-peer-deps` removal per Phase 7

One architectural deviation was captured and approved mid-execution: **Phase 6 D-01 Amendment** upgraded `eslint-plugin-vue` from `^6.2.2` to `^9.33.0` because v6 used a removed ESLint 9 internal API (`codePath.currentSegments`) that made it incompatible at runtime. The amendment enabled LINT-03 to land as originally scoped in Phase 7 (no `--legacy-peer-deps` deferral to v2.0).

- Premise: CONTEXT D-01 declared "no plugin upgrades" in Phase 6 based on research finding that `eslint-plugin-vue@6` would load under ESLint 9 with only peer warnings.

- Resolution: User approved upgrading `eslint-plugin-vue` to `^9.33.0` (still Vue-2-compatible). CONTEXT.md updated with D-01 Amendment. Cascade: D-15 Option 1 (persist `--legacy-peer-deps`) became obsolete because the vue-plugin upgrade cleared one of the two peer conflicts, making LINT-03 achievable as originally scoped in Phase 7.
